Should I repair or replace my car? The UK guide for 2026

Facing a large repair bill is one of the most stressful car ownership moments. The gut reaction is often to replace the car — but that's not always the right financial decision. This calculator and guide will help you make the choice based on numbers, not emotion.

The 50% rule — a useful starting point

The most widely-cited rule of thumb is the 50% rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the car's current market value, you should seriously consider replacing it. So if your car is worth £5,000 and you're facing a £2,800 repair bill, the maths starts to favour replacement. However, this rule ignores several important factors including reliability, mileage, running costs, and how long you plan to keep the vehicle.

When repairing is the right choice

  • The repair cost is under 30% of the car's value
  • The car has a strong service history and has been reliable until this point
  • The repair fixes a single, well-understood problem (not a symptom of broader wear)
  • You have no outstanding finance on the vehicle
  • The replacement cost would require taking on new debt
  • The car has relatively low mileage for its age

When replacing is the right choice

  • The repair cost is over 50% of the car's current value
  • The car has had multiple expensive repairs in the past 12 months
  • High mileage means further repairs are likely regardless of this fix
  • The repair is for a safety-critical system (brakes, steering, suspension)
  • Running costs are high and a newer car would be significantly cheaper to run
  • You need a more reliable car for work or family commitments

Don't forget the hidden costs of replacing

The sticker price of a replacement car is rarely the true cost. Factor in: higher insurance for a newer vehicle, road tax changes, the cost of financing if you take a loan or PCP, any early settlement fees on existing finance, and the fact that a new-to-you car will also depreciate. Our calculator includes all of these in the total cost of replacement figure.

Get a second opinion on the repair quote

Before making any decision, always get at least two quotes from independent mechanics. Main dealer quotes are typically 30–60% higher than independent specialists for the same work. The AA and RAC also offer vehicle inspection services if you want a professional assessment of your car's overall condition before deciding whether to repair or replace.
