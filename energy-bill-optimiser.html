How to reduce your energy bills in the UK in 2026

UK energy bills have remained elevated following the energy crisis of 2022–2023. While prices have stabilised, the average UK household still spends around £1,500–£2,200 per year on gas and electricity. The good news is that a combination of switching, insulation, and smart usage can cut bills by 20–40% for most homes.

The fastest way to save: switch your energy supplier

The energy price cap means all suppliers charge the same unit rates, but tariffs with discounts, fixed rates, and smart tariffs can still deliver savings of £100–300 per year for an average household. Green energy suppliers like Octopus Energy consistently rank highly for both price and customer service, and offer time-of-use tariffs like Octopus Agile that can slash bills for flexible households.

Smart meters — free and worth it

Smart meters are free to request from your energy supplier and typically reduce bills by 5–10% simply through greater awareness of usage. They also enable access to smart tariffs, including overnight cheap-rate electricity ideal for EV charging and storage heaters. Contact your supplier to book a free installation.

Insulation — the biggest long-term saving

Loft insulation and cavity wall insulation are the two highest-impact home improvements for energy efficiency. A poorly insulated home can lose up to 25% of heat through the roof and 35% through walls. The Great British Insulation Scheme offers free or subsidised insulation to households on lower incomes or with an EPC rating of D or below — many homeowners are eligible and don't realise it.

Solar panels in 2026 — are they worth it?

With installation costs falling to £5,000–£9,000 for a typical 3-bedroom home and electricity prices remaining high, solar panels now typically pay back within 7–10 years. The Smart Export Guarantee (SEG) also pays you for excess electricity exported to the grid. With a 25-year panel lifespan, the long-term return is compelling for most south-facing properties.

Quick wins that cost nothing

  • Reduce heating by 1°C — saves approximately £80–100/year
  • Switch to LED bulbs throughout — saves £40–60/year
  • Turn off standby appliances — saves £30–50/year
  • Wash clothes at 30°C instead of 40°C — saves £20–30/year
  • Use a dishwasher at full load on eco mode — saves £20/year vs hand washing
  • Draught-proof doors and windows — saves £50–100/year
